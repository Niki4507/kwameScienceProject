Series Connection



How Does This Work?

This works because when the bulbs are connected in series, the individual charge has to pass through each of the light bulbs consecutively in the series.


In a series connection, as more bulbs are added circuit, each consecutive bulb will become gradually dimmer because the current in the circuit is decreasing.


If a bulb is broken or a wire breaks, the current stops at that broken section, and the remaining bulbs do not light up.
